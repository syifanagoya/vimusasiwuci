Early Life & Biography

Cybill Lynne Shepherd was born in Memphis, Tennessee, on 18 February 1950. Her mother, Patty Shobe, was a homemaker while her father William Jennings Shepherd worked as a manager at a home appliances shop. She attended East High School, where she took part in beauty contests, and she won the “Miss Teenage Memphis” title. She also represented the city during the Miss Teenage America in 1966, and she won the congeniality award. In 1968 she competed for the “Model of the Year” title, and afterward, she became a fashion star.

Personal Life

Cybill met her first husband, David M. Ford, in 1978, and they began dating and got married the same year. David was a nightclub entertainer and also a local auto parts dealer. In 1979 the couple welcomed their first child Clementine Ford, and in 1982 they divorced.

Cybill married Bruce Oppenheim in 1987, and they were blessed with twins; Cyrus Zachariah and Molly Ariel. After only three years in marriage, the couple divorced. She got engaged to Andrei Nikolajevic in June 2012, and after dating for three years, they called it off in 2015.

Career

Her following roles were not successful, and they included in At Long Last Love, Daisy Miller, and The Lady Vanishes. Her career became a failure, and she had to go back to her hometown, where she worked on regional theatre.

She returned to New York in 1982, and she immediately appeared on a theatre tour of Lunch Hour. Luck started shinning on her again in 1983 when she got a role in the night-time drama titled The Yellow Rose. Her career was defined by a role she got in the series Moonlight on ABC, which aired from 1985 to 1989.

In 1990 she reprised her role in the sequel to The Last Picture Show. Other appearances were on Alice, Once Upon a Crime, Martha: Behind Bars, and Do You Believe? Other than films, Cybill has appeared in several television films like Cybill, 8 Simple Rules, Psych, and No Ordinary Family, among others. She has also appeared in the Criminal drama Mind and The L Word.

Her other albums were At Home With Cybill, Jazz Baby Volumes, Talk Memphis to Me, and several others. As a model, she appeared on the cover of various magazines, including the Glamour Magazine in 1970. She also posed for the manufacturers ok Kodak as the Kodak Girl in 1972.

Awards & Achievements

Cybill was nominated for Emmy Awards in 1986, 1995, 1996, and 1997 for Outstanding Lead Actress in Drama and Comedy Series.
