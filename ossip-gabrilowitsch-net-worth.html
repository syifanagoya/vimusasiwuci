Role in Founding the Detroit Symphony Orchestra

Ossip Gabrilowitsch was born on February 7, 1878, in Saint Petersburg, Russia, into a Jewish family. A pivotal figure in the classical music scene, Gabrilowitsch’s founding of the Detroit Symphony Orchestra marked a significant chapter in his career.

Under his leadership, the orchestra not only flourished but became a cornerstone of cultural life in Detroit. His insistence on a new auditorium led to the creation of the Orchestra Hall, a venue that remains iconic.
