Is Erik Hooks Treason Linked To His Arrest? Mugshot And Charges

Erik A. Hooks, the Deputy Administrator of the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), found himself at the center of a storm as the United States Marines arrested him on treason charges.

The arrest, on Saturday, August 19, sent shockwaves through the nation, raising questions about the alleged sinister deeds that led to this dramatic development.

According to a 34-page indictment, Hooks and his superior, Deanne Criswell, purportedly issued orders to FEMA agents stationed in Maui that raised serious concerns about their actions during a devastating fire crisis.

The indictment alleges that the duo instructed agents to deny essential supplies like food and water to fire victims, obstruct civilian relief efforts, prevent displaced individuals from leaving the fire-ravaged region of Lahaina, and even dispose of bodies in a way that raised suspicions of impropriety.

Sources close to the situation have revealed that a source in General Eric M. Smith’s office informed them of these allegations.

Hooks’ arrest unfolded at his residence in Arlington, Virginia, near FEMA’s headquarters in Washington, D.C.

Marines apprehended Hooks while he was asleep, presenting him with a military arrest warrant outlining the charges against him. Subsequently, he was taken into custody for processing.

According to the sources, one piece of central evidence to the case is a typewritten memo that reportedly bears the signatures of both Hooks and Criswell.

This memo allegedly encourages FEMA supervisors in Maui to dispose of deceased individuals discreetly, either through incineration or secretive burials.

While the memo does not explicitly instruct the execution of civilians, disturbingly, Marines on the scene have claimed to discover numerous bodies with gunshot wounds consistent with small arms used by FEMA and the FBI.

Erik Hooks Education And Career

Erik Hooks, hailing from Spring Lake, North Carolina, has carved a distinguished path through education and a dynamic career.

He kickstarted his academic journey by securing a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science and government.

Eager to expand his knowledge, he pursued a Master of Liberal Arts from North Carolina State University, further enriching his intellectual repertoire.

Hooks’ foray into public service began in 1989 when he joined the North Carolina Department of Public Safety.

He demonstrated his capabilities, eventually becoming the assistant special agent in the Special Investigations Division in 1999.

Hooks received an appointment in January 2017 and was chosen by Governor Roy Cooper to serve as the secretary of the North Carolina Department of Public Safety.

He carried out with dedication until his retirement in July 2021.

2021 marked another pivotal chapter in Hooks’ career as he became the Deputy Administrator of the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA).

President Biden and subsequent confirmation by the U.S. Senate nominated him.

Beyond his core responsibilities, Hooks shoulders the crucial mantle of Key Intelligence Official at FEMA, tasked with overseeing Homeland Security Information and National Intelligence.

His role extends to active participation as a voting member in both the Homeland Security Intelligence Council (HSIC) and the Counter Threat Advisory Board (CTAB).
