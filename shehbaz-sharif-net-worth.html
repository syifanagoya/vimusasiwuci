Shehbaz Sharif Career

Shehbaz Sharif Career: - Shehbaz was chosen to serve in the Punjab Provincial Assembly in 1988 and the Pakistan National Assembly in 1990. In 1993, he was once more chosen to serve in the Punjab Assembly and was appointed Leader of the Opposition. On February 20, 1997, he was chosen to serve as chief minister of Punjab, the province with the largest population in Pakistan. After the PML-N won the Punjab province in the 2008 Pakistani general election, Shehbaz was elected Chief Minister for a second term. In the general election of 2013, he was chosen to serve as Punjab's Chief Minister for the third time. He held the position till his party was defeated in the general election of 2018.

After his brother Nawaz Sharif was barred from office as a result of the Panama Papers scandal, Shehbaz was proposed to lead the Pakistan Muslim League-N. Following the 2018 election, he received a nomination for Leader of the Opposition. Shehbaz was taken into custody by the NAB at the Lahore High Court on September 28, 2020, and he was charged with money laundering. He was detained while waiting for a trial. In a money laundering case, the Lahore High Court granted him bail on April 14, 2021. On April 11, 2022, following the motion of no-confidence against Imran Khan, he was chosen as prime minister amid the political unrest in Pakistan of that year.
